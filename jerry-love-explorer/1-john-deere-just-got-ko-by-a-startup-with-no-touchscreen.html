WHY IS THIS BLOWING UP?

1
The "Right to Repair" War
John Deere locks tractors with proprietary DRM — farmers can't fix their own machines without paying thousands for an authorized tech. Ursa Ag bypasses this entirely with an open, unrestricted alternative.
2
MTTR — Mean Time to Recovery
During harvest, downtime = lost crops. A mechanical Cummins engine can be fixed on the spot with a wrench in 30 minutes. Modern tractors have millions of failure points (sensors, ECUs, telemetry).
3
The "Anti-Complexity" Backlash
Tech workers are exhausted by "smart" TVs, touchscreen cars, and appliances needing WiFi. A simple machine that just works — no subscriptions, no telemetry, no forced updates — is deeply appealing.
4
The Open-Source Hardware Dream
Engineers don't hate tech — they hate locked-down tech. Buy a $95K "basic" tractor, then add your own open-source iPad system and Raspberry Pi sensors. It turns a tractor into a platform.
5
Classic David vs. Goliath Disruption
John Deere profits by complicating products. A lean startup cuts the price in half by eliminating unnecessary complexity. Textbook Silicon Valley disruption model.
