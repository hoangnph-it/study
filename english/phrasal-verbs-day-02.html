WHAT ARE PHRASAL VERBS?

01
VERB + PREPOSITION
A phrasal verb = verb + preposition (in, on, out, up, away). E.g., walk out, sit down, speak up.
02
OFTEN IDIOMATIC
The meaning is different from the individual words. "Speak down" = talk to someone as if they're less intelligent.
03
SPEAKING & WRITING
Usable in both. If you learned it by listening → spoken. If by reading → written. Keep it simple.

SEPARABLE vs INSEPARABLE

Separable: Object can go between verb & preposition — "I look a word up" or "I look up a word"
Pronouns MUST go in the middle: "I look it up" — NOT "I look up it"
Inseparable: Verb & preposition stay together — "I take after my father" (never "I take my father after")
No rules to tell which is which — you must learn each one by heart from context & dictionary examples

BATCH 1 — WORK & STUDY (PART 1)

01 KNUCKLE DOWN
To start focusing on your work or study. "Exams are coming, so I really need to knuckle down."
02 GET ROUND TO
To finally do something you've been too busy for. "I'd love to learn cooking but I haven't got round to it."
03 WHILE AWAY
To spend time in a relaxed way. Separable. "I love to while away the afternoon watching movies."
04 BRUSH UP ON
To improve a skill you haven't practiced for a while. "I need to brush up on my guitar playing."

BATCH 2 — TALKING ABOUT THE FUTURE (PART 3)

05 BANK ON
To expect and hope something will happen. "Many governments are banking on the economy improving."
06 BE IN FOR
About to have or experience something. "I think we are in for a few surprises in the future."
07 PAN OUT
To result or develop in a certain way. "We'll have to see how things pan out."
08 RUN UP AGAINST
To face problems or difficult situations. "We're going to run up against a lot of problems in this area."

BATCH 3 — GIVING OPINIONS (PART 3)

09 — MULL OVER
Think carefully about something. Separable. "I've been mulling over this a lot recently." — Great trick to buy thinking time!
10 — FIGURE OUT
Find the solution to something. "It's not easy to figure out a solution." — Then carry on giving your answer.

BATCH 4 — GOVERNMENT QUESTIONS (PART 3)

11
GET ON WITH
Start or continue doing something. "The government just needs to get on with it."
12
BUCKLE DOWN
Start working hard. "It's time the government buckled down and found a solution."

BATCH 5 — DESCRIBING THINGS & PEOPLE (PART 2)

THINGS
13 — STUMBLE UPON
Find something by chance. "A great book I stumbled upon recently."
PEOPLE
14 — BUMP INTO
Meet someone by chance. "I bumped into an old friend last week."
RESULT
15 — TURN OUT
End up / result in a certain way. "In the end, everything turned out fine."

PRONUNCIATION POWER TIPS

Consonant → Vowel Linking
Many phrasal verbs let you link sounds: look‿up, sit‿up, speak‿up. Show off your control!
3rd Person Present
"He looks‿up words all the time" — the /s/ links smoothly into /ʌ/.
Past Tense Linking
"She looked‿up a lot of words" — the /t/ links into the vowel naturally.
bumped‿into / turned‿out
Past tense phrasal verbs are perfect for showing smooth connected speech.

KEITH'S KEY ADVICE
Learn phrasal verbs in context, not from long lists.
Learn 5 at a time. Know if each is separable or inseparable. Practice in real conversations until they sound natural. Unnatural use has a negative effect — only use ones you're confident with.

PHRASAL VERBS = BAND 7-8 VOCABULARY

They make you sound natural in both spoken and written English.

Build a base of basics, then add less common idiomatic ones to impress your examiner.
