AUSSIE ENGLISH PHRASAL VERBS

PHRASAL VERBS THAT

AUSSIES ACTUALLY USE EVERY DAY

by Minh Quân · Vietnamese-Australian English Teacher

GO OFF 5 MEANINGS
🧀
Food goes bad / spoils
"If you don't put the milk in the fridge, it will go off."
⏰
An alarm or bell rings
"My alarm went off this morning and woke me up."
😤
Someone loses their temper
"My husband went off at me last night because dinner was late."
💥
Something explodes
"The bomb's about to go off!"
🎉
An event or party is really fun (informal)
"The party is going off!" / "The wedding went off last night!"
CHECK OUT 4 MEANINGS
🏨
Leave a hotel
"Let's check out of our hotel room."
📚
Borrow books from a library
"I checked out three books from the library yesterday."
👀
Look at something cool
"Check out that amazing sunset!"
😍
Look at someone you find attractive
"I saw you checking out that girl." / "Did you see those guys checking me out?"
STRIKE OUT FROM BASEBALL
⚾
To fail — especially when trying to flirt or approach someone
"I struck out with Jessica — I don't think she liked me."

Origin: In baseball, when the batter misses the ball 3 times = strike out (eliminated).

PICK UP 3+ MEANINGS
🚘
Give someone a ride / collect something
"Can you pick me up at 5?"
📈
Improve / increase / gain momentum
"If you study more, your scores will pick up." / "My YouTube views are starting to pick up!"
😘
Successfully flirt with someone (opposite of strike out)
"He picked up that girl at the party."
GANG UP SOCIAL
🤝
Everyone teams up against one person
"They ganged up on me." / "Let's gang up on Steve!"

Similar: "team up" (but without the negative connotation).

BONUS: THE "_____ OUT" FORMULA

Create your own phrasal verbs to express being sick of something:

CREATE YOUR OWN PHRASAL VERB
[Thing you're sick of] + -ed / -d + OUT

When you do something too much and you're over it — slap "out" on it!

Sick of working
WORKED OUT
"I'm so worked out today."
Sick of burgers
BURGERED OUT
"We had burgers 3 days in a row — I'm burgered out."
Sick of YouTube
YOUTUBED OUT
"Been watching all day — totally YouTubed out."

⚠ Very informal — use in friendly conversation only, not in writing tests!
